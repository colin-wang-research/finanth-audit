\section{Conclusion}
FinAuth-Audit makes benchmark validity part of financial-agent authorization evaluation. Its frozen controlled test shows that low raw FAR can coexist with authority laundering, while provenance integrity, delegation coverage, utility, missed opportunity, and review load form a genuine frontier. A larger prospective actual-proposal test does not reproduce the inverse rank transfer observed in a 60-date pilot; it instead separates economic loss, material harm, authority violation, and development-only recalibration. The powered order-book test retains a mixed intersection-union failure, while public-power and training-coverage gates keep weaker evidence exploratory or \textit{N/A}. The \href{https://github.com/colin-wang-research/finanth-audit}{public artifact} preserves each evaluation surface and its adverse results. FinAuth-Audit therefore supports reproducible comparison of execution rules across weak financial priors without treating prediction quality, any model source, any baseline, or any single metric as execution authority.
