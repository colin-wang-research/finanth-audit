# Submission Scope Freeze

## Title

FinAuth-Audit: A Validity-Aware Benchmark for Financial Agent Authorization

## Identity

The paper evaluates whether authorization benchmarks can make rules look safe
through coverage collapse, provenance laundering, non-point-in-time public
transfer, or shortcut-driven training utility.

## Main research questions

1. coverage collapse;
2. provenance laundering;
3. point-in-time controlled-to-public transfer;
4. benchmark training utility.

## Main figures

1. raw versus certified ranking;
2. provenance laundering over role noise and hop depth;
3. controlled-to-public cluster-aware transfer;
4. D0-D7 training utility.

All four figures must be new and script-generated. No AAAI figure, old rank
reversal figure, or clean-role leakage figure may be reused.

## Main contribution exclusions

- no new execution-authority algorithm;
- no EPV in title, abstract, or contributions;
- no AAAI theorem;
- no MiniWoB, coding, or tool experiment;
- no EPV-centered result ordering;
- no claim of deployment, investment profitability, or human study;
- no FinAuth-Worlds hidden-period result;
- no arbitrary scalar leaderboard score.

## Paper-writing gate

The abstract findings and Results narrative must not be drafted until the four
RQ experiments, certification audit, cluster statistics, negative-results
check, and overlap audit have completed. Task, protocol, metric, and artifact
sections may be scaffolded earlier.
